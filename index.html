About DNA and RNA

DNA and RNA are nucleic acids that store genetic information. DNA contains the bases A, T, G, and C, while RNA contains A, U, G, and C. In DNA, Adenine (A) pairs with Thymine (T), and Guanine (G) pairs with Cytosine (C). In RNA, Thymine (T) is replaced by Uracil (U).

  • A: Adenine (A) is a purine base that pairs with Thymine (T) in DNA and Uracil (U) in RNA.
  • T: Thymine (T) is a pyrimidine base that pairs with Adenine (A) in DNA.
  • G: Guanine (G) is a purine base that pairs with Cytosine (C) in both DNA and RNA.
  • C: Cytosine (C) is a pyrimidine base that pairs with Guanine (G) in both DNA and RNA.
  • U: Uracil (U) is a pyrimidine base found only in RNA, pairing with Adenine (A).
